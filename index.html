Ashley Thomas Barnes

I have recently started an astronomy research position at the Institut für Theoretische Astrophysik, Zentrum für Astronomie der Universität Heidelberg. My research focus is on understanding the star formation process -- with particular interest on high-mass stars -- within the range of environments present across the Milky Way. I conducted my PhD studies between Liverpool John Moores University and the Max Planck Institute for Extraterrestrial Physics. During which time I investigated differences in the kinematic and chemical properties of gas on the verge of forming stars within Galactic Centre and the Spiral Arm. It appears that the gas within the Galactic Centre is more extreme, harbouring densities, turbulence, pressures -- key properties for star formation -- orders of magnitude higher than Spiral Arm star-forming regions. In light of this, I determined the star formation rate within this extreme environment, and find a potential deviation from the current theoretical models for star formation. This investigation made use of observations taken with a variety of telescopes (interferometric and single dish) over a range of wavelengths (millimetre, sub-millimetre radio to infrared).
